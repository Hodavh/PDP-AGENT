1
Requirements & Discovery
"Define what good looks like before building further"
Days 1–7
▼
📋
Define PDP Quality StandardsResearch

Review 10–15 of Protein Works' highest and lowest converting PDPs to establish what 'good' looks like in this specific brand context. The nine rubric dimensions draw on industry research but need calibrating to Protein Works' price tier, customer demographic, and product categories.

Standish CHAOS Report — incomplete requirements contribute to 60%+ of failed IT projects
⚖️
Map Compliance by Product CategoryCompliance

A greens powder, a collagen supplement, and a pre-workout have different claim risk profiles under the GB NHC Register and MHRA borderline product guidance. Map the compliance landscape category by category rather than applying a single uniform standard.

CAP Code Section 15 · MHRA Guidance · GB NHC Register
🎯
Define Success CriteriaDiscovery

Establish measurable success criteria before the system produces output — not after. What recommendation acceptance rate constitutes success? What conversion lift would justify continued investment? These numbers must be agreed before week 3 testing.

McKinsey — projects with defined success criteria are 2.5× more likely to deliver on objectives
🤝
Marketing Team BrainstormWorkshop

The marketing team are the primary users. Run a structured session covering: current PDP analysis workflow and where it breaks down; what an ideal system would produce; what would make them trust its output enough to act on it; and how the approval and publishing workflow actually works today.

IDEO Participatory Design Research — people involved in creating a system are significantly more likely to adopt and advocate for it
🏗️
Map the Approval WorkflowDiscovery

Understand how a recommendation moves from suggestion to live page change. The output format must slot into the existing workflow rather than creating new steps. Who approves compliance-sensitive copy? Who signs off on pricing changes? These gates define the human-in-the-loop design.

2
Architecture Review
"Validate technical decisions against real constraints"
Days 8–14
▼
⚡
Evaluate Latency vs Quality Trade-offArchitecture

The current pipeline produces an audit in 3–8 minutes. Acceptable for manual use, potentially not for catalogue-wide automation. Establish the acceptable latency threshold per use case and identify which pipeline stages to optimise if the threshold is not met — the Rewriter is the most expensive step.

💰
Validate Cost Model at Intended ScaleArchitecture

At ~£0.004 per audit, full catalogue coverage costs under £5/month. Confirm the intended scope across markets (UK, Ireland, Germany, France) and product variants, then validate the cost model at that scale before committing to an architecture that assumes single-market use.

🖥️
IT Department Integration MeetingIntegration

Meet with IT to understand the data landscape: which analytics platforms are in use (GA4, Adobe, Shopify, internal BI), data governance policies around storing scraped content and reviewer names, and infrastructure standards (AWS, Azure, GCP). The answers determine whether SQLite is appropriate or whether the system connects to existing data infrastructure from day one.

📊
Plan Real Analytics ConnectionIntegration

The business impact dashboard currently runs on sample data. Identify which real analytics data is accessible and how to connect it — GA4 API, Shopify analytics export, or internal BI tool. The dashboard only becomes a genuine business tool when it shows actual conversion data, not illustrative figures.

🔒
Security and Compliance ReviewSecurity

Confirm data retention policies for scraped page content and reviewer names under GDPR. Plan API key management for production (secrets manager vs environment variables). Define the URL allowlist to prevent SSRF attacks and ensure the system only audits Protein Works domains.

3
Build & Internal Testing
"Build the version the team will actually test"
Days 15–21
▼
🎛️
Calibrate the Rubric to Protein WorksDevelopment

Update scoring criteria and thresholds to reflect what good looks like specifically for Protein Works' product categories and brand voice — not the generic DTC ecommerce standard the prototype was built against. Category-specific rubric variants for greens, protein, collagen, and pre-workout.

📝
Align Output Format to Existing WorkflowDevelopment

Restructure the recommendation output to map directly to the team's briefing templates, copy approval process, and project management tool. The system should produce output the team can act on without translating — not output they need to reformat before using.

🧪
Internal Testing on Real PDPsTesting

Run the system against 5–10 Protein Works PDPs across different product categories and price tiers. Evaluate three things: are recommendations specific and grounded (not generic); are compliance flags accurate against the GB NHC Register; does the overall score correlate with known page performance from analytics.

🔍
Manual Compliance ValidationCompliance

Manually review every compliance flag produced across the test set against the CAP Code and GB NHC Register. False positives — flagging compliant copy as risky — erode trust faster than false negatives. Calibrate the compliance dimension until the false positive rate is under 10%.

📈
Set Up LangSmith EvaluatorsObservability

Configure offline LangSmith evaluators to measure recommendation grounding, compliance flag accuracy, specificity, and predicted acceptance rate. These run weekly against a reference dataset of past audits to catch quality regression as prompts evolve — measurement infrastructure, not a real-time guardrail.

4
User Feedback & Refinement
"Earn the team's trust through structured iteration"
Days 22–30
▼
👥
Marketing Team Feedback SessionFeedback

Present 3–5 worked examples on real Protein Works pages. Collect structured feedback: rate each recommendation on usefulness and actionability; identify which to implement immediately versus park; flag compliance suggestions that seem incorrect. This structured feedback becomes the training signal for prompt refinement.

🎯
Measure Against Success CriteriaEvaluation

Assess the system against the criteria defined in week 1. Measure recommendation acceptance rate — the percentage the team said they would implement. Measure compliance flag accuracy against manual review. Assess whether output format fits the existing workflow without requiring translation.

🔄
Targeted Prompt RefinementDevelopment

Based on feedback, make targeted adjustments to the Actor and Rewriter prompts. Which recommendation types consistently get rejected? Which compliance flags are always disputed? These patterns drive specific prompt changes — not a general rewrite of the system.

🚀
Define Handover and Ongoing OwnershipGovernance

By day 30, agree who owns the system going forward: who monitors LangSmith for quality regression; who updates the rubric when best practice evolves; who manages the compliance claim list as regulations change. An AI system without a named owner degrades silently.

✅
Go / No-Go Decision★ Milestone

Day 30 outcome: the system either meets the agreed success criteria and moves to production use, or specific gaps are documented with a remediation plan before scaling. The goal is not a perfect system — it is a system the team trusts enough to use on real pages consistently.

🔐
Automation — Phase 2
Unlocked when team trust is established
Future Phase
🔄
Scheduled Catalogue ScanningAutomation

Automated weekly scan of all Protein Works PDPs across all four markets, prioritised by traffic and conversion data. Flags pages that have degraded below a score threshold since the last audit.

🔗
Publishing Workflow IntegrationAutomation

Connect approved recommendations directly into the content management pipeline. Accepted recommendations create draft copy changes in the CMS for editor review — eliminating manual copy-paste from audit output to CMS.

🧪
A/B Test OrchestrationAutomation

Automatic A/B test creation for implemented recommendations, with statistical significance monitoring. Replaces estimated impact scores with measured conversion lift — closing the feedback loop between recommendation and outcome.

🧠
Continuous Prompt ImprovementAutomation

A/B test outcomes feed back into the rubric and prompt calibration. Recommendations that consistently produce conversion lift get higher impact scores. Recommendations that produce no measurable lift are deprioritised. The system gets smarter with every test.
